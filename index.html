When Marnie Was There

The film tells the poignant story of Anna Sasaki, a young girl struggling with feelings of loneliness and alienation. Sent to the countryside to stay with relatives, Anna discovers an abandoned mansion and encounters a mysterious girl named Marnie. As their deep friendship unfolds, the film delicately explores themes of self-discovery, acceptance, and the powerful impact of genuine connections. With breathtaking animation and a heartfelt narrative, "When Marnie Was There" is a captivating exploration of friendship and the transformative power of understanding oneself and others.

Arrietty

The film is based on Mary Norton's novel "The Borrowers" and offers a unique perspective on the world through the eyes of tiny beings living beneath the floorboards. The story revolves around Arrietty, a small borrower, and her family who live by borrowing small items from the "human beans" in a suburban home. As Arrietty befriends a human boy named Sho, the film beautifully explores themes of friendship, trust, and the delicate balance between the miniature world of the borrowers and the vast world of humans. With its meticulous animation and heartfelt narrative, "Arrietty" captivates audiences with its whimsical storytelling and evocative portrayal of a hidden, magical realm.

Ponyo

The film presents a whimsical tale centered around Ponyo, a curious and adventurous fish who befriends a young boy named Sosuke. As Ponyo dreams of becoming human, her magical transformation disrupts the balance of the ocean, leading to a series of fantastical and heartwarming adventures. Set against a backdrop of vibrant underwater landscapes and backed by Joe Hisaishi's enchanting score, "Ponyo" beautifully explores themes of love, friendship, and the interconnectedness of nature.
